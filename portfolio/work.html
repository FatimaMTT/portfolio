Long shot made for an assignment for my Foundations in Visual and Media Arts Class.

Description of the assignment:
30 seconds minimum, 2 minutes maximum. No editing. Silent.
In a single shot, create a portrait of a place or object through the “lens” of a specific emotion or feeling. There can be people in your shot, but they should not be the main subject of your piece. Don’t focus on what the objects/people can do to create the emotion, focus on what the camera can do. Use composition, the lens, and the camera settings to be evocative. The camera can move or be still, zoom and/or focus can change or remain constant, etc.

Description of my project:
The feeling that I was striving for was cold, and to get this feeling I went to the lake in the commons that was frozen. There were ducks in that lake, and I decided that it would work better to shoot the ducks once the lake was not frozen anymore. I think that humans always see animals as very calm creatures who have no worries, especially creatures like ducks that just chill in the water. So my feeling changed to calmness, so I used the zoom of the camera to shoot only the ducks and the water, which was what, for me, showed the most calmness. I also didn’t move the camera to emphasize the calmness.
